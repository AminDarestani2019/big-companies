Pixar is an acclaimed animation studio known for its groundbreaking CGI films. Founded in 1986, it originated as a division of Lucasfilm before being acquired by Steve Jobs. Pixar’s debut feature, Toy Story, was the world’s first fully computer-animated movie. The studio is known for technical excellence and emotionally rich storytelling. Its films, including Finding Nemo, Up, and Inside Out, have won numerous Academy Awards. Pixar’s RenderMan software set industry standards in animation. It maintains a strong creative culture and collaborative environment. The studio is now a subsidiary of The Walt Disney Company. Pixar's influence on modern animation is unparalleled. It continues to push narrative and technological boundaries.
