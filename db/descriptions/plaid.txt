Plaid is a fintech company that connects consumer bank accounts to apps like Venmo and Robinhood. Founded in 2013, it provides APIs that enable financial apps to access user banking data securely. This data powers services like budgeting, lending, and investment. Plaid plays a critical role in the financial ecosystem by enhancing transparency and access. It supports developers with tools to build next-gen financial services. The company was nearly acquired by Visa, but the deal was canceled due to antitrust concerns. It serves both consumers and institutions. Plaid emphasizes data security and regulatory compliance. Its mission is to democratize financial access. It enables innovation in personal finance.
