Hermès is a French luxury fashion house established in 1837, known for its craftsmanship and exclusivity. It specializes in leather goods, accessories, perfumes, and ready-to-wear. The brand is iconic for its Birkin and Kelly handbags. Hermès maintains tight control over its supply to enhance brand prestige. Products are handmade, contributing to high prices and limited availability. It resists mass-market trends, focusing instead on timeless elegance. The brand remains family-owned, allowing it to maintain long-term strategies. Its artisans undergo years of training. Hermès is a symbol of ultimate luxury and heritage. Its branding emphasizes refinement, tradition, and artisanal skill.
